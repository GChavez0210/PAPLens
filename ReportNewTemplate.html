How key metrics are calculated

Average AHI
sum(nightly AHI) / number of nights
Clinical value: tracks residual sleep-disordered breathing burden under therapy.
Adherence Rate
nights with usage >= 4h / analyzed nights x 100
Clinical value: low adherence can explain persistent symptoms despite otherwise adequate settings.
Leak 95th Percentile
95th percentile of nightly leak values
Clinical value: high leak can reduce delivered pressure and event detection accuracy.
Correlation r
Pearson coefficient from -1.00 to +1.00
Clinical value: shows what variables move together. Correlation does not prove causation.

Bring these questions to your clinician

  1. Are my residual events mostly obstructive, central, or mixed based on this event profile and trend charts?
  2. If leak is elevated, should we adjust mask type/fit before pressure setting changes?
  3. Does my pressure pattern look reactive (events first, pressure second) or insufficient baseline support?
  4. Given adherence and symptom history, what is the most useful next intervention before my next follow-up?
